What is Scalp Micropigmentation (SMP)?

SMP is the process of applying natural pigment to the scalp to replicate the look of hair follicles. It creates the appearance of fuller, thicker hair.

About Me

Your trusted SMP specialist

Every restored hairline has a story. This is mine. Hi, I'm N2 Tobi, the artist behind INKROOT | Hairline Architect. Based in Calgary, I specialize in Scalp Micropigmentation (SMP), a precise technique that restores the appearance of a natural hairline for individuals experiencing thinning hair or hair loss. My journey began in the world of tattooing and fine line artistry, where I was drawn to structure, depth, and detail. Over time, that passion evolved into Scalp Micropigmentation —a discipline that combines technical precision with subtle artistry. As the founder of INKROOT, I approach every SMP session with intention, balance, and care. For me, it's not just a cosmetic service - it's restoration. Quiet, confident, lasting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common SMP questions.

SMP is the process of applying natural pigment to the scalp to replicate the look of hair follicles. It creates the appearance of fuller, thicker hair.

Most clients describe SMP as having minimal discomfort, similar to getting a tattoo.

SMP is non-surgical, requires no downtime, and doesn't rely on donor follicles.

Typically, 2-3 sessions are recommended for optimal results, with refresh touch-ups every 1-3 years.
